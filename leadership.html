The operation of the Hoboken Volunteer Ambulance Corps depends on the success of both its members and its leadership. Below are some of those in our organization involved in running the organization.

Thomas F. Molta is the President of the Hoboken Volunteer Ambulance Corps. He retired from the Hoboken Fire Department at the rank of Captain in 2011 after serving for 25 years. As the President, Tom is ultimately responsible for all actions of this organization, and represents the Corps in meetings with outside parties. He also serves as our public information officer. Tom has been a member of the Hoboken Volunteer Ambulance Corps for over thirty years and has held an EMT-B certification since they started offering them.

    Key Stats

  • Joined corps on October 2, 1980
  • President since 1993
  • Hazmat and Incident Response Instructor

Mark Harris is the Captain of the Hoboken Volunteer Ambulance Corps. Mark works full-time as an engineer doing nerdy things that only nerds do. As the Captain, Mark's primary duty is overseeing the day to day operation of the corps. Mark has been with the Hoboken Volunteer Ambulance Corps since 2003.

    Key Stats

  • EMT since 2003
  • Has also been 1st Lieutenant and 2nd Lieutenant of Vehicles
  • Holds Bachelor's and Master's in Electrical Engineering from Stevens Insitiute of Technology
  • Handles application and interview process for corps

John T. Mercun is a crew chief with the Hoboken Volunteer Ambulance Corps. John works full-time handling marketing for a publishing company. As a crew chief, John's primary responsibility is overseeing duty crews and ensuring proper procedures and patient care. John has been with the Hoboken Volunteer Ambulance Corps since 2008.

    Key Stats

  • EMT since 2001
  • Assists with new member orientation as well as EMT field training
  • Holds Bacherlor's and Master's from Fordham University
